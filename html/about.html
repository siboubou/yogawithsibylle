My Yoga Journey
I discovered Yoga while travelling around South America.
Passing by Hare Krishna’s Temples, I was initiated to Yoga and to the Vedic Philosophy. Since, the concept of “all is one” grew inside me along with the practice of yoga.
Yoga gave me peace, but also a structure.
It is a philosophy as much as it is a practice.
A philosophy of acceptance, devotion, union. A practice of discipline and letting it go.
Not only I could feel the physical benefits on my body, stronger, more energized, also I started to feel more grounded and connected.
Altogether, I felt better!
This is what I want to share through Yoga and why I have started teaching it.
Interested in a holisitc approach of the body and how to take car of it, I started practicing Energetic Therapies three years ago.
In 2019, I graduated of 200H Yoga Teacher Training of Vrinda Bhumi School in India, where I also learned some of the basis of Ayurveda and Ayurvedic Massages Saatwika Ayurveda Academy.
Combining Meditation and Energetic Therapy, I got rid of my hyperphagia and it helped me a lot going out of depression and changing my perspectives. Anytime I am feeling down I know I can turn towards those tools
I genuinely want people to feel good, which is why I started offering energetic cares.
How I Teach
My approach into sharing yoga is to make you listen to your own body. Instead of trying to reproduce what you may think is "The way to do it" or a perfect shape, you'll get to connect with yourself. Your shape is what matters, and how you evolve with and within it.
As Every Body Is Different, Each Practice is Unique
You will dive into deep breathwork, make your energy flow easily through you, release tensions. I will guide you into cultivating body awareness, pointing out micro adjustments you can create in a posture, giving you the understanding of where to take the energy from and how to create space in your body.
I give importance to the core and pelvic work as it is fundamental for good posture and to relief backpain.
Yoga is for everybody !
I cannot stress that enough ! Do not bridle yourself : you don't have to be already strong, or flexible… You'll eventually get to it by practicing Asanas consistently but that's not the only point for practicing yoga.
Connection, of body, mind and spirit is what is seeken.
So you just have to be you, willing to inner grow, opening your body, your mind and your spirit. Not that's anything easy… Sometimes laying ourselves bare can be challenging, here is when the practice makes you elvove.
Own your practice, it is yours !
How I Practice
Some days I like to flow improvising new Vinyasas, some other days I follow a strict Hatha pattern. Sometimes I add up reps or fascia work techniques, and other times I just melt in a Yin practice of restoration. Days I just breath consciously and this is all.
